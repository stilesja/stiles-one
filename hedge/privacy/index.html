Privacy policy

Last updated: May 12, 2026

The short version

Hedge collects no data. Everything stays on your phone.

The long version

What we collect

Nothing. There are no user accounts, logins, or profiles. Your answers, scores, calibration data, and streaks are stored in a local SQLite database on your device. They are not transmitted anywhere and we cannot see them.

Network access

Hedge does not transmit your data to any server. The app makes no network requests for analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, advertising, or any third-party service. New question batches may be delivered via Expo Updates over your normal device connection, but no information about you is sent in those requests beyond what the operating system itself transmits when checking for updates.

Notifications

If you opt in to daily reminders in Settings, Hedge schedules local notifications on your device. These do not require an account and do not send any data off the device — iOS handles the scheduling locally.

Cloud sync

Hedge does not sync your data to iCloud or any other cloud service. If you reinstall the app or move to a new device, your play history does not transfer.

Third-party SDKs

Hedge is built with Expo and React Native, plus a small set of open-source libraries for fonts, icons, and on-device audio playback. None of these libraries collect or transmit user data in the way Hedge uses them. Hedge includes no analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs.

Children

Hedge is rated 4+ and contains no advertising, in-app purchases, or user-generated content. It is safe for children but does not specifically target them. We collect no data from any user, regardless of age.

Changes to this policy

If Hedge ever starts collecting data — for example, if optional iCloud sync is added — this policy will be updated and the change announced in the app's release notes before the new version ships.
