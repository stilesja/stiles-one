Hedge: Build Log

Engineering events documented as they happen. The evergreen architecture picture lives in the case study; this is what actually happened along the way.

  • July 21, 2026 The first honest witness

    Two devices agreed and were both wrong. The Mac, with no dev-era past, exposed a stranded-history sync bug; SQLite timestamps read like a flight recorder, and a zone-rebind fix healed every device in five minutes with zero interaction.

  • July 20, 2026 Nobody had walked this path, so here's the map

    CloudKit from an Expo app, pothole by pothole: simulator entitlements that lie, a zone API that succeeds while failing, radioactive dev data, and the gitignore supply-chain bug that shipped a build without its own native module. Ends with the extracted open-source bridge.

  • July 19, 2026 The schema had already done it

    Adding iCloud sync to a no-backend app: immutable answers, derived state, and deterministic shuffles turn out to be the sync design, waiting. Plus the chip-economy bug this very write-up caught before it published.

  • July 18, 2026 The compositor doesn't care how pretty it is, only how big

    An iPad button lag found by feel: decorative background gradients drawn as screen-diagonal squares, four giant layers per transition. The classic caching fix made it worse; shrinking the work by eighty times fixed it. Plus the pressed-state test, no profiler required.

  • July 17, 2026 iPad support in a day, and then every screen Apple sells

    A morning "would iPad be hard?" reaches App Store approval the next day: the compatibility-mode trap, thumbs-as-design-constraint, five flushed-out bugs, a screenshot heist, and flipping the switches for Mac and Vision Pro.

  • July 16, 2026 The countdown was centered, and Todd was right anyway

    A tester said the countdown digits did not feel centered, and the measurements said he was wrong. He was right twice: a shrink animation that dragged the digit upward, and the anatomy of the numeral 1.

  • July 12, 2026 Six plans, one day, and an agent fleet

    Executing a quality campaign with Claude sub-agents: two-stage reviews, 123 new tests, a same-day feature from watching real players, and a self-inflicted publish outage on the way out.
