Frequently asked

Can I replay a question after answering it?

No. Once you commit an answer, it's locked. Replays would destroy the calibration signal — the whole point of the game is to learn what your confidence actually means, and that only works if every answer counts.

What happens if I miss a day?

Your streak survives one missed day as a grace period — play "today" by the end of the day after, and the streak stays alive. Two missed days in a row breaks it.

How is the score calculated?

Hedge doesn't just check whether you got the answer right. It checks whether you got the confidence right. The math is called Brier scoring, and it's designed to punish bullshit confidence and reward honest uncertainty.

Every question is worth between 0.00 and 1.00 points. You earn the most when you're right AND confident. You lose the most when you're wrong AND confident. Hedging at 50% earns 0.75 either way — that's your honesty baseline.

5 questions per round → max score is 5.00, baseline (all 50% guesses) is 3.75.

Why doesn't a correct answer always score 1.00?

Because Hedge measures calibration, not just accuracy. A correct answer at 99% confidence scores ~1.00. A correct answer at 50% confidence scores 0.75. Same outcome, different bets, different rewards.

The formula:

score = 1 − (your_confidence_in_the_truth − 1)²

If you said TRUE at 80% and the answer was TRUE, your confidence in the truth was 0.80, so:

score = 1 − (0.80 − 1)² = 1 − 0.04 = 0.96

You bet 80% and won — close to perfect, but not max because you held back 20% of the bet.

Why does 50% still earn points (0.75)?

Because admitting "I have no idea" is a correct description of your knowledge state when you have no idea. Honest calibration is rewarded.

50% confidence in Hedge means "I'm flipping a mental coin." If your coin flip happens to be right, that's luck — Brier doesn't pay you extra for it. If it's wrong, you weren't really wrong about anything you claimed to know — Brier doesn't punish you either. Both outcomes score 0.75.

That 0.75 is the honesty baseline. You only beat it by knowing more than nothing, and you only fall below it by betting confidence you didn't have.

Why is being confidently wrong so brutal?

A wrong answer at 99% confidence scores ~0.00 — basically nothing.

score = 1 − (0.01 − 1)² = 1 − 0.98 = 0.02

Hedge is built on the idea that "I'm sure" should mean "I'm sure" — and getting that wrong is genuinely diagnostic. Over many rounds, those zero-point losses tell you which categories you systematically overrate yourself in. That's the whole calibration story.

What's a good score?

Hedge classifies each round into one of these tiers at the celebration screen. The rules check whether you got all 5 right FIRST, and only fall back to the score threshold if you didn't:

WhenTier
5/5 correct at 99% confidence eachMaximum
5/5 correct, any lower confidenceFlawless
Not 5/5, score 4.00 or higherStrong Round
Not 5/5, score 3.75 – 4.00Room to Grow
Score under 3.75Oof

3.75 is the baseline — what you'd score if you bet 50% on all 5 questions. Anything above it means real signal. And getting all 5 right always counts as Flawless or Maximum, even if you hedged the confidence — because the math only tracks the gap between your bet and the outcome, and "right" closes that gap regardless of how big the bet was.

Notice how the 50% row is symmetric — the only place where correctness doesn't matter. Above 50%, being right pays more and being wrong costs more, and the gap widens fast.

Hedge scores your bet more than your answer. Bet what you know, hedge what you don't, and the math will keep you honest over time.

What does the calibration chart mean?

The chart on the Stats screen plots your accuracy at each confidence level against the perfect-calibration reference line (y = x). Bars above the line mean you're underconfident — your 70%s are actually 85% right. Bars below mean overconfident — your 90%s are only 65% right. Over time, the chart tells you whether you trust yourself too much, or not enough.

Will there be more questions?

Yes. New batches are pushed via Expo Updates, so you don't need to manually update the app to receive them.

Does Hedge work offline?

Yes — once you've launched the app at least once, all gameplay is local. No internet required.

Will my data sync across devices?

Not yet. iCloud sync is on the future-features list. For now everything is stored locally per-device.

How do I delete my data?

Uninstall the app — that's it. All Hedge data lives in the app's local container on your device, and iOS deletes it when the app is removed. There is nothing to delete on any server because nothing is stored on any server.
